Background Information

The Fatimah Al-Zaharah Orphanage Complex (KAYFAZ) was established on a 3.5-acre piece of land in Lot 1120, Bukit Katil District, Melaka. The land was generously donated as a waqf by Mrs. Wan Hajah Che Mah binti Hitam to the Melacca Islamic Women's Welfare Board "Lembaga Kebajikan Perempuan Islam Melaka" (LKPI) with the aim of creating a space for orphans. The ownership of the complex rests with LKPI, while its administration and operations are overseen by the Melaka Islamic Religious Council.

KAYFAZ officially began its operations in December 2004 and was formally inaugurated by the Honorable Governor of Melaka on March 16, 2006. Presently, the orphanage provides a home for 85 students, specifically catering to orphans and underprivileged children from the state of Melaka.

The orphanage, managed by the Melaka Islamic Religion Council, serves as a haven for unfortunate girls without families or those from economically disadvantaged backgrounds. Its capacity accommodates 80 children at a time, with 37 in primary school and 43 in secondary school.

The facility boasts a library stocked with hundreds of books catering to various age groups, including reference books, Malay and English storybooks, and exercise books for exam preparation. Additionally, there is a computer lab on the first floor, though it currently lacks internet connectivity, limiting its use to typing tasks.

Under the leadership of Datuk Hj, Md. Yunos Bin Husin, the Melaka Islamic Religion Council manages the orphanage, with Mdm Hjh. Rasimah Bt Puasa serving as the chairperson responsible for administration. Six additional staff members contribute to the efficient operation of the orphanage.

Situated in Tebing Tinggi, Bukit Katil, Melaka, the orphanage is conveniently located near Masjid Jamek Bukit Katil. Accessible by following the directions to Bukit Katil, the orphanage was constructed on a lot generously donated by the landowner to the Melaka Islamic Religious Council.
